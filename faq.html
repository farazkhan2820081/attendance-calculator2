Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about attendance calculation and our tools.

General Questions

What is the 75% attendance rule?

The 75% attendance rule is a common academic policy requiring students to attend at least 75% of their classes to be eligible for final examinations. This means out of 100 classes, you must attend at least 75 classes to meet the minimum requirement.

How do I calculate my attendance percentage?

To calculate attendance percentage, use this formula: (Classes Attended ÷ Total Classes) × 100. For example, if you attended 45 out of 60 classes, your attendance percentage would be (45 ÷ 60) × 100 = 75%.

No, we don't store any of your personal attendance data. All calculations are performed locally in your browser, ensuring your privacy.

Tool-Specific Questions

Can I use these tools for multiple subjects?

Yes! Our Class Attendance Tracker is specifically designed to help you monitor attendance across multiple subjects simultaneously and calculates your overall attendance percentage.

What's the difference between the Required Attendance Calculator and Bunk Calculator?

The Required Attendance Calculator tells you how many classes you need to attend to reach a target percentage, while the Bunk Calculator tells you how many classes you can skip while maintaining your minimum required percentage.

How accurate are the calculations?

Our calculations are mathematically precise and based on standard attendance formulas used by educational institutions worldwide. The results are rounded to appropriate decimal places for practical use.

Academic Policies

Do all institutions follow the 75% rule?

While 75% is the most common requirement, different institutions may have varying policies. Some require 80% or 85% attendance. Always check your specific institution's attendance policy.

What happens if I don't meet the minimum attendance?

Consequences vary by institution but may include:

  • Ineligibility for final examinations
  • Automatic course failure
  • Requirement to repeat the course
  • Academic probation

Can medical leave affect my attendance?

Most institutions have provisions for medical leave that may not count against your attendance. However, policies vary, so check with your academic office for specific guidelines.
